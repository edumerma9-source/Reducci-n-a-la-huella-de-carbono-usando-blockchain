# Eco-Carbon UNMSM: A Blockchain Incentive Protocol for University Sustainability

> **Innovating for a Sustainable Future Hackathon 2025**
> Transforming environmental conservation into a verifiable economic asset within the Universidad Nacional Mayor de San Marcos.

---

### 1. Solution Summary (Abstract)
Eco-Carbon UNMSM addresses recycling market failures through an EVM-compatible smart contract deployed on the LNet blockchain. By encoding the reward mechanism directly into transparent, immutable code, the protocol collapses transaction and enforcement costs. The on-chain, auditable record functions as a self-executing, Pigouvian-style subsidy that internalizes the positive externality of recycling.

### 2. Alignment with Hackathon Objectives
The protocol advances the "Innovating for a Sustainable Future" directive by leveraging decentralized architecture for environmental sustainability.

* **Objective 1 - Market Friction Elimination:** Establish an incentive-compatible economic mechanism that minimizes transaction costs.
* **Objective 2 - Open-Source Public-Goods Scalability:** Deliver a non-rival and non-excludable codebase that can be adopted by other institutions at a near-zero marginal cost structure.

### 3. Project Name
The project is formally designated **Eco-Carbon UNMSM**. The name communicates the design logic: a direct, on-chain link between a pro-environmental action and a verifiable economic reward.

### 5. Problem Description & Market Failure Analysis
Peru faces a structural crisis in solid-waste management. In 2024, only 2.7% of waste was effectively recycled. From a microeconomic standpoint, this is a market failure driven by **negative externalities** (uncompensated social costs) and high **transaction costs** (Coase, 1960), compounded by **information asymmetry** (Akerlof, 1970).

### 8. Justification of Impact and Scalability
Eco-Carbon UNMSM functions as a signaling mechanism (Spence, 1973). On-chain records provide a credible signal of environmental behavior, resolving information gaps and enabling secondary use cases like reputation scoring. The near-zero marginal cost (MC → 0) allows for rapid institutional scaling.
